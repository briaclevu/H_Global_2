Notebook to explore and visualize change in H+ amplitude (ΔH+) in CMIP5 scenarios using PyFerret

Following the study of LE VU et al, in prep. about the acidification in Mediterranean Sea lets have a look on the amplitude change - if any - in the CMIP5 ptojection for 2100. Start with IPSL.

Define H+ and ΔH+ present and futur

In [5]: 
%%ferret
let H1=10^(-1*PH[d=2,l=1:12])*1e9
let H2=10^(-1*PH[d=3,l=1:12])*1e9
let D_H_2006=H1[l=@max]-H1[l=@min]
let D_H_2106=H2[l=@max]-H2[l=@min]

Plot surface delta amplitude of H+ in 2006 and 2106¶

In [6]: 
%ferret_run -q -s 500,300 'shade/levels=(0,10,0.5) D_H_2006' % locals()
%ferret_run -q -s 500,300 'shade/levels=(0,10,0.5) D_H_2106' % locals()

Plot ratio between 2106 and 2006 amplitude (<1 it's a decrease) and relative change (<0 it's a decrease)¶

In [7]: 
%ferret_run -q -s 500,300 'shade/levels=(-1,5,1) D_H_2106/D_H_2006' % locals()
%ferret_run -q -s 500,300 'shade/levels=(-1,5,1) (D_H_2106-D_H_2006)/D_H_2006' % locals()

Firts conclusion

  • There is general increase in amplitude between 2006 and 2100 similar to the one in the Mediterranean Sea (a doubling)
  • We observe in high lattitude and frontal area (South Iceland, Southe Africa, South Indian and Arctic) a important increase (5 fold the 2006 amplitude)
  • Sometimes there is a decrease (south Pacific, Antarctic,...) but seema adjacent to an important increase area which could be due the interannual variability

Perspective

  • The same study with decadal monthly climatology is needed to smooth the interannual variability
